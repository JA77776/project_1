The Founder & CEO

Loretta Joseph is an internationally recognized social activist, trainer, and development expert with over 35 years of experience.

She embodies a life of balance—dedicated to her family, her work, and the broader community. Guided by her golden rule, “Treat others as you wish to be treated,” Loretta continues to inspire change and lead with compassion.

The Story of WE CAN

Our History

Hailing from Matroosfontein Parish, actively involved in social justice projects with the Dominican Nuns from Ireland, born in a family deeply rooted in Catholic doctrine, working cold face at community level, the disparities/inequality of injustice could clearly be seen in the various communities the Dominican Nuns were working in at a spiritual level. Aspiring to live Christ-like, Loretta Joseph (Nee Burgers) was drawn in by the nuances that continue to create social disruptions, be it in communities, schools or in families.

With her father Norman Burgers' support, Loretta embarked on a groundbreaking research study (2005-2006) in the Western Cape's Cape Metro, focusing on the Eastern and Northern regions. Her investigation explored the correlation between domestic violence and its far-reaching consequences on women's well-being, family dynamics, and interpersonal relationships.

Following the heart-breaking car accident that took the lives of Loretta’s father, sister-in-law, nephew, and niece on 23 January 2007, Loretta honored her father's memory by registering the charitable organization he had unwaveringly encouraged her to establish.

As the cherished daughter of her father’s unconditional love, Loretta drew strength from her mother's inspiring sayings, such as "Action speaks louder than words." These words of wisdom fostered resilience, determination, and faith in God, empowering Loretta to bring her charitable vision to life.

Growing up, Loretta was frequently reminded of her mother Ida Burgers' demonstrative phrase, "Life and death go hand in hand." This insightful saying has become a guiding principle, underscoring the inevitability of life's cycles.

Women Empowered Committed Against Negativity came into fruition and has been practicing as a Social Justice organization since. November 21, WE CAN will be celebrating its 18th Anniversary.

International Day Of the Girl Child 2020 (IDGC)


31 October 2020 On this day we celebrated IDGC, where we invited 50 girls from six different communities to gather and share a meal together. Not only did they share a meal, they shared their knowledge with each other. The aim of this event was to give our girls the platform to raise their voices and listen to what is happening in other communities. The girls heard that most of them are facing the same challenges in their community.
